Project Description

I was approched by ABSA and RBB to take an already conceptulised board game, that would be used as an internal training tool. This would introduce newly recruited staff -both managers and branch owners to particpant in a printed boardgame. The board game would educate, enrich and engage new staff members and Operational managers how to manage and maintain thier individual branches. This would further be used and implemented towards the inner workings and day to day challenges that they would face while operating thier branches.

The board games colour palette was strictly ABSA CI documentation. All items that were requested included. The iconography of the the game cards, the Play booklet and the board game itself all of these items used their CI and colour palette as illustrated above. The Iconography that was created required a construction guideline, as this was a request in initial brief that was supplied.

The Iconography design for all items both on the board game and individual items this included the board game itself, the game cards the player tokens and the Play book were designed with the above construction grid in mind.

The game cards were split into four sets. Each represented a different challenge and or tasks that were required to be completed by the players. The iconography that was illustrated on the face of the cards, would visually display what the category of the task or challenge they were to face, This was a requirement set durning the initial brief and concept phase.
